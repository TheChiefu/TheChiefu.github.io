Mid-Year Update & First Japan Trip

Written: 2023-06-17

Change in Japanese Goals

Originally this year I’d planned to get to level 50 in Wanikani by the end of this year then take the JLPT N2 test in December. The problem being, is that I did not anticipate how much burnout I’d experience so soon in my studies. I should’ve known from experience that going at a fast face would lead to burn out but at the start of the year I was doing 2 levels of Wanikani in a month by rushing 100s of new lessons in a day, studying multiple times a day, and so on. But by the time April came around I had flat-lined completely on Wanikani. To make matters worse when I got my wisdom teeth taken out I was drained of all my energy for weeks.

That’s not to say I didn’t do anything between then and now, just that my Japanese reviews had dropped and am now revising my goals. So from now on instead of chasing level 50 in Wanikani to get 80% JLPT N2 coverage, I’m now aiming for 8,000 words on Anki (already halfway there) and N3 coverage on BunPro, while dropping Wanikani entirely. Although it pains me to do so, I’ve been noticing a trend throughout my studies that Wanikani has a severe flaw that other SRS tools like Anki and BunPro don’t have. It’s called “Burned” items.

To quickly summarize, “Burned” items are items you’ve seen multiple times and got correct for at least 6 months. The assumption made is that if you got it correct that many times you “should know it by now”. This assumption according to multiple posts on the Wanikani forums by the creator himself is that you should already be consuming Japanese content and already be seeing the words you’ve encountered on the site elsewhere. While it’s a noble assumption, I’m in the “not so lucky” group that doesn’t actively like studying the language. So what ends up happening to me is that I study almost exclusively on Wanikani for months, get a word burned, never see it again and forget it. Meaning that I have a list of words from at least a 1-2 years ago that I haven’t seen and likely already forgot. This is the biggest reason I’m cutting my losses with Wanikani. It’s been a boon in kick-starting my Kanji comprehension and I’ll give credit where it’s due, but I feel like I’ve fallen outside the tool’s useful group.

An example of a word I burned back in 2021 that I didn’t know today. This would of been sent back as a “new” word in Anki, but if I kept up with Wanikani I’d never see it again.

This is where my continuation of Anki and BunPro come into the picture. With those SRS apps they’ll show words from any time period. I’ve come across words I haven’t seen in a year or two, and if I forgot it I can simply press “forgot” and it pushes the review into the “relearning” phase. Where in which it shows me the word frequently until I build up the correct percentage again. This is great for someone like me who doesn’t do (really any) consumption of Japanese content outside a few niche areas since the tool doesn’t assume I “just know it”. I’ve often had periods where I’m relearning a couple old cards and getting it wrong helps me remember it better because now it sticks out. Plus I was doing a lot of “double reviewing” in that words from Anki and Wanikani overlapped. So now I can save time by just doing one. BunPro on the other hand is purely grammar, so I can use words I learned in Anki on that site. Which lets me synergize between the two tools.

To wrap my Japanese goals up, I’ve been planning on studying the rest of my Anki cards to completion. Progressing through grammar reviews on BunPro (adding any unknown words from there to anki) until I do the N3 tier. As well as start playing games in Japanese (I’ve been playing the new Zelda game in Japanese) to build up the connections between what I’m studying and content I’m consuming. Who knows, once I get my number of daily Anki reviews to a low enough number (10s) I might come back to finish off Wanikani (since I did pay for a lifetime subscription back in 2019).

I’ve also been seriously looking into getting a tutor for speaking and listening practice since I get zero of that personally. Also since I’m planning of moving to Japan end of next year I need to build up that skill now rather than later. Speaking of Japan, I already booked a flight this year to visit again and haven’t told of my first trip to Japan that I did last year.
