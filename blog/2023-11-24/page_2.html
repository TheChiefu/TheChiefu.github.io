End-Year Update

Written: 2023-11-24

The Travel Bug

Nothing much in my life was drastically different around this point. I was getting into more Japanese listening practice, getting some JLPT prep books, and ordering some stuff to send back to RI. Mostly gaming peripherals and such so that when I visited for a week or two I’d have a similar setup to my AZ one. Around this time I planned to visit Peru for an extended weekend and Mexico for another one since I had a new goal of visiting every continent at some point in my life.

Peru Trip (July 2023)

In July I made the trek out to Peru and landed in Cusco after an agonizing 14 hour flight, multi-hour layover in LA Airport and changing gate numbers. After more than 24 hours of being awake, I stood outside the Cusco airport waiting for my taxi. I was greeted by what looked like some lady and her kid asking me if I was the person they were assigned then took a quick ride from the Airport to my hotel. I knew going in it was going to be sketchy since Peru isn’t the most well off economically, but I used the few Spanish words I knew to thank the driver and check myself in. The hotel itself was okay, but I usually don’t care about accommodations, as long as I had hot water to shower in.

Shortly after checking in I got in and readied myself for a shower. I saw a big brick missing from the bathroom wall, and just took a shower since I was too tired to care. It was only like 3PM local time but I was tired and pretty much slept until like 8PM. I booked my hotel next to the train station that’d take me to the town closest to Machu Picchu so that I didn’t have to travel far to the station which was at least relieving.

Around 5:20AM I went to leave my hotel but noticed that everything in the hotel was turned off and locked. The reception had no one there and the front door had a steel door covering it from the outside. At first I was freaking out since I wouldn’t be able to catch my train in time, defeating the entire reason I came to Peru. But luckily a younger man who looks like he helps run the place with his family came out of a backroom to open the door for some of his friends to get in, and I took that time to leave and head off.

I luckily got to the station in time and took a short bus ride from it to another station an hour away, from there I took the train to the summit near Machu Picchu. I’d seen some tall mountains before, especially when traveling across the US from east coast to west; but the mountains here were mindbogglingly tall. I usually don’t take vertical pictures but I pretty much had to in order to encapsulate the sheer scale of these mountains. Once arriving at the summit town I went to the bus stop that transports people from the town to the Machu Picchu entrance and took the winding roads all the way up to the top.

River between the town of Aguas Calientes

I arrived on time and went on my route given for the day I arrived. I saw the usual sights and luckily a few German guys that were there helped take a picture of me in front of one of the iconic spots. I saw the huge mountains, old sights, tourists stopping in the middle road to take selfies; the usual. I took a few panoramic shots to test out if I could do it or not; luckily it worked and have some high quality surround pictures. Shortly after finishing the route I took the bus back to the summit town, ate some local food and waited around 5 hours until my train ride back to Cusco arrived. I bought a few souvenirs, watched Star Wars on my phone, and finally got on the train. It took a few hours to get back to the transfer station and when I got there had to wade through hoards of people to get to the bus station. Once I got there I got on, took a seat, and sighed a breath of relief since I wasn’t sure if I was going to get on in time because the previous train ride was half an hour delayed. The bus ride back was nice since it was already night out and I had a window seat. I saw all the stars in the sky like I never had in a long time. By 9PM I arrived back at Cusco and went to bed.

The next day I had free and decided to order some take-out to bring back to my hotel. I took a good half hour walk to pick it up and came back. I regretfully forgot to ask for utensils and had to resort to eating noddles with my hands (I don’t recommend this). But once I was done I washed my hands, watched some videos throughout the day and prepared to fly back the next day. I then flew back to LA, to Phoenix and the short ride home. I still had the travel bug in me and immediately started plans to visit Mexico and the pyramids there.

Mexico Trip (August 2023)

In August during another extended weekend I took a short direct flight from Phoenix to Mexico City. I heard of a lot of bad things about crime there so I booked a capsule hotel in the Airport and locked my stuff in a locker. I wasn’t paranoid since I remember reading that of all the places to be in Mexico, Mexico City was the safest; but I at least wanted to have my wits about me. Nevertheless I got my metro card when I arrived and reviewed my route to the pyramids before I went to bed. The next morning I took the train to the bus station that ferries people to the pyramids and then took the bus on an hour or so ride to them.

Capsule Hotel in Mexico City

When I arrived I could see the pyramids right from the street, the scale of them was massive. I knew I wasn’t able to capture them in one photo, so I took tons of panoramics and stitched the image together. I walked around the sights reading the signs/history and taking in the fact that I was standing on the same site as thousands of Aztecs before me. It was a little disappointing that I couldn’t walk up the pyramids because they now banned it, but understand for preservation purposes why they did that. I took a couple hours to walk around and funnily enough overheard a Japanese tour going on; so I listened for a bit until I went my own way.

The sky was pretty clear and I felt lucky because the night before I was reading that rain was coming. I got my photos then left before the rain would ruin it. I bought some small souvenirs since I packed a small bag on my trip and couldn’t bring back much; then took the bus ride back to the airport. I ate some dinner at the airport then spent the night in the capsule hotel, listening to the rain come in and soothe me to sleep. The next morning I took the flight back to the US and planned my Europe trip. However some unexpected changes happened shortly after.
