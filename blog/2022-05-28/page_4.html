Dreams & Doubts

Written: 2022-05-28

Concluding Thoughts

This leaves me to where I am today, confused and scared about my future and goals. I wish I could end this post on a more happy note but I’m not going to lie about my self. I wrote in my last post that I was battling with depression and lying to myself wouldn’t help.

I haven’t completely given up hope on my dreams to make a game, learn Japanese, and move abroad but waiting for years and years, wasting my life away for dreams hurts a lot. The dreams that I’ve described in this post have been life long dreams that I’ve had for as long as I can remember, and in 10+ years I’ve achieved zero of them.

I remember the last time I talked to my mother about my dreams, she said that “You’ve been waiting your whole life to do them” and realizing how true it was. As a young kid I just enjoyed the things I like and thought nothing of them. But as I got older I felt like because I wanted to make a game I needed to make one; because I wanted to move to Japan and learn Japanese that I had to do it.

When I was graduating high school I pushed back studying abroad in Japan because it was expensive (2017), then in college pushed back studying abroad again due to covid (2020), then when I graduated college pushed back moving to Japan 3 years since I felt inadequate with my Japanese (2022). I even had the thought the other day that I might need to wait until 2025-2026 to move to Japan with how slow my Japanese is progressing. What’s stopping me from wasting more of my life in limbo for a dream I may never achieve?

I may be in a period of growth and I’ll finally achieve one or many of my goals in the future, but I earnestly feel that I have to let go of one of my dreams to not let depression worsen due to sense of failure. I can’t spend my life worrying about my goals otherwise I’ll have this mental fog my entire life, and that’s not a life I want to go though.

So for a while or until I move to Japan I’ve been thinking of letting my game dreams die off. Those things made me happy in the past and excited, but now all it does is bring me sadness so it’s best to leave it in the past. At least I can make measurable progress in Japanese with enough brute force and will.
