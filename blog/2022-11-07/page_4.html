Half Western and Half Eastern

Written: 2022-11-07

Introduction of Japanese Media through the Web

Growing up I was around my parents and grandparents listening to classic rock, watching movies from all eras, playing games, etc; the usual childhood for someone in America. However Japanese culture seemed to seep it way into almost every aspect of my life, unknowingly through localized versions of Japanese games via Sony and Nintendo consoles, Anime through Cartoon Network; as well as though Japanese games shows online and on TV. It’s not uncommon to know people who grew up in the 2000s and has had a slight interest in Japanese culture though these means.

Early in life I was really into Japanese game shows and would find as many clips and shows as I could. My favorites being “Hole in the Wall” (originally known as Brain Wall / 脳カベ ) and Silent Library ( ダウンタウンのガキの使いやあらへんで! ). Where the former is where a giant foam wall with a cutout is pushed towards a contestant that needs to somehow take the pose of the cutout and not get pushed into a vat of water, while the latter is a show where contestants try to stay silent and not laugh while various wacky activities go on around them.

There are a plethora of other game-shows I watched hours of when I was younger. But over time as I searched the internet for various things such as game and TV show soundtracks more and more Japanese media seemed to make its way to my attention. I started to discover music from Japan and being amazed by the different styles and mood they could evoke.

The earliest music I got into being fan created songs from a famous game called Touhou (東方). Unbeknownst to be me at the time, this game series spawned countless circles or サークル (groups of people who create fan content related to a piece of media; ie. game, show, etc) who create endless amounts of remakes and covers. The earliest ones I remember being these ones:

  • SILVER FOREST – つるぺったん
  • Ten Incredibly Soothing Touhou Arrangements
  • 【東方二次創作アニメーション】 東方活動写真館
  • Soundholic – Piere (プリエール-)
  • Touhou Symphonic Orchestra
  • IOSYS – Alice→Dere

And of course the originals that they are based off of are also good:

The Touhou community was really the gateway to me exploring more into Japanese internet culture and therefore music and media. Over time expanding to anime soundtracks, games soundtracks, and etc from numerous games. I even got into Jazz, a genre I didn’t really care about at first through the wonderful mix of western cowboy culture and future Japanese space operas in the form of the show Cowboy Bebop ( カウボーイビバップ ); such as this catchy song: Too Good Too Bad.

Around 2014 to 2017 was really the era in which I discovered an interest in almost every genre of music, since Anime and Japanese games seemed to be so creative in their usage of other culture’s music. If I didn’t tell anyone that these songs: Étoile Et Toi, Blumenkranz, Komm Süsser Tod were from anime or even Japan no one would of guessed.

Funnily enough learning more about Japanese culture has introduced me to other more cultures around the world. Which makes sense since Japan after the opening of their borders in 1853 their culture has shifted from being isolationist to more “inclusionist”. More specifically they still retain their cultural roots but take and morph aspects other cultures into their own to grow theirs. So you get these weird combination of cultures mixed into one product, and it creates wonderful pieces of art.

When I got into watching Studio Ghilbi movies I was into the time periods and people that those movies portrayed. Even though fictitious most of the movies from that studio are based on 1920s-1980s depictions of western and Japanese life. The movie Porco Rosso takes place in the Adriatic sea in 1929, From Up On Poppy Hill takes place in 1963 Japan during it’s time of economic recovery, The Tale of Princess Kaguya is a recreation of an old Japanese tale from the 9th-10th century called The Tale of the Bamboo Cutter, and finally the movie called The Wind Rises takes placing during 1940s Japan during the creation of the Zero fighter jet, and the real creator’s (in this movie a fictitious depiction) life during that period.

Although not the point of these movies, I like seeing depictions of life before my own time to see what people were like and acted like; and for some reason Animation helps transport me to those time periods. I feel like the scenery and human nature portrayed in animation connects with me more, since seeing actors makes it feel more “modern”, while an artistic interpretation feels more close grandiose and meaningful. And since we don’t have real or color photos from times like the 9th century and so on, seeing depictions of those times is more awe inspiring. This is just my introduction to Japanese media through the web, Japanese music has also played a huge part in my life.
