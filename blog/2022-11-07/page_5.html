Half Western and Half Eastern

Written: 2022-11-07

Influence of Music

I’ve always been into orchestral music, but I feel like Japanese composers use a combination of the “old/new” to create unique and interesting music. Especially composers for movies and video games using music to really sell a world and environment. When I hear this soundtrack from the “Tale of Princess Kagyua”, I feel like I could close my eyes and feel like I’m back in that time period experiencing what life was like in a simpler part of history. In instances of modern games and anime, composers are really creative with the usage of orchestra and modern instruments to create otherworldly soundtracks.

I could go on and on about just that one composer alone and I wouldn’t be doing Japanese composers enough credit. There is just so much variety in the Japanese music industry it’s hard to even know where to start. So I’ll move on from just one composer and focus on a variety of artists.

One trend that helped me find more Japanese artists happened back in 2016-2017 when YouTube was recommending users “City Pop” music. “City Pop” is a modern term for what is basically just 1970s-1980s J-Pop music and this is where my interest in Japanese music started to meld with my interest in western classical rock. Like I said before, since I grew up with the culture and music from my parents and grandparents generation, this was the meshing of both words and I discovered numerous artists that I still listen today.

One of my favorite artists being Yumi Matsutoya (松任谷由実): a 1970s-2000s singer and national icon in Japan. She has hundreds of songs spanning over 40 years of her career, and my favorites are from her earliest works in the 70s. My jaw dropped when I first heard the first song in “The 14th Moon” album 朝日の中で (In the rising morning sun):

Listening to this for the first time I was in awe, frozen with joy in what I was hearing. It was the perfect blend of orchestra and soft rock elements packaged into one, and reminded me of the music “The Carpenters” used to make.

I actually found her music through two places without even knowing who it was until I finally started looking for more of her music. The first through a Studio Ghibli anime movie trailer called “The Wind Rises” where her 1973 song ひこうき雲 / Vapor Trail is used as the backing track. As well as again through a live concert recording posted to YouTube. Ever since I started looking into her music it reminded me of the later Beatles which incorporated orchestral music in songs such as: The Long and Winding Road, Carry That Weight, and She’s Leaving Home. The combination of rock elements with orchestra has always been a favorite of mine.

Which lead me down a rabbit hole of Japanese artists from various eras.

Suffice to say Japanese music has played a big role in my life. I listen to it to put me into a good mood, go through and process heavy emotions when in a bad mood, and overall use it as a tool for my mental health. Since nothing helps more than listening to a song or piece that connects with you emotionally. Speaking of emotional media, anime has was also a driving point in inspiring interests in my life.
