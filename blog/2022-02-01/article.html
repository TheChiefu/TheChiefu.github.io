Today starts the beginning of an unplanned yet exciting journey. “What is that journey” you ask? Well, starting today I (Austin Lanphear) have decided to create a website that acts as both a personal website where I can update on personal topics but also a landing page for Japanese learners to access useful resources.

I’ve had a personal website for a while but never really did anything with it because it was basically just a digital resume, which LinkedIn basically does anyways. So I’ve decided after the 4 years I’ve had it that I’ll just transfer the old posts to this website under my “Personal Blog” tag and just keep any updates about my personal self there. The difference this time around on this website is that I still can use it as a personal website but now have more use of it as a utility for both myself and others.

It’s a personal utility because I can put non-sensitive information like my preferred programs lists (for when I reinstall/install an OS to new computers), game ideas / white-boarding, etc on the website without needing to create copious amounts of backups to my phone, google drive, etc. Since all the managing of the data is done by WordPress and their servers; so I don’t have to worry about copying/using old backups or whatever.

It’s also a utility for others since I can provide a place for learners (of anything really, but primarily Japanese) a central place to gather and use resources from. One being just a place of general links and two being my review/critique of resources. I personally believe writing about the resources with their pros/cons is useful since a info-dump of random links doesn’t really mean anything if you have no idea what they are even for.

An example of a review would be something like this:

Wanikani – A Kanji learning platform where you start on a predefined track of kanji and vocabulary.

Pros: Covers all of the Joyo-kanji, plus 6600 words

Cons: Predefined track of Kanji/Vocabulary you have to take in order independent of current Japanese level

~ Austin Lanphear

This removes any ambiguity of what each resource and link is about since I tell you how and why I listed it in the first place. As a software developer I know from experience how infuriating bad documentation or no documentation is. So when I suggest resources or information to people I want to make sure they are well information to make their use of the information easier to digest.

Well that pretty much wraps up the first introductory post to this website, have fun browsing. The website may be incredibly sparse for a few days as I set everything up and get my writing brain in order, but I intend to update it frequently for the first month or so.
