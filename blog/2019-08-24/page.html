It’s Been A While…

Written: 2019-08-24

I’m going to be honest and state right off the bat that I completely forgot about this site for a bit. I think in an earlier blog post I said I’m not good at keeping up with things I post online up to date. But nevertheless I’m posting today about some very crucial changes I’ve been making not only to Taurus but to my personal life that effect my development process.

Making Amends to Myself:

So one big issue I have with doing any project is keep up with it. I always go full throttle into a new project whenever I’m in good spirits and I keep up with that until inevitably and understandably get burnt out. I’ve known about this issue of going balls to the wall (as coined in 1960s aviation) for a long time; 2015 or so. Back when I was working on my campsite in my aunt’s backyard. I’m in “go go go” mode until I hit a hurdle; then I try to figure out the hurdle but in the process of figuring out how to overcome the hurdle I tire myself out. Sometimes I overcome the hurdle and sometimes I don’t, but either way I’m too mentally tired to continue after I overcome it. Then when I come back to the project I either have no idea what I was doing and don’t know how to go back to a project without restarting or just give up.

Burn Out and Multiple Ventures:

Taurus is a prime example of this phenomenon; starting, restarting, stopping again, restarting, ever since 2017 on and off. I’ve heard good advice to never or try not to start multiple projects but I can never seem to follow that advice. Since I have so many ideas floating around in my head that I don’t want to throw away, I always write them down, start a new project, or etc; and in the process I start numerous projects and to this day have never finished any. Privately I’ve been working experimentally on a Halo-type shooter but for a bit I messed around with NavMesh components in Unity for AI navigation since I figured that multiplayer games are infinitely more complicated than single-player (in terms of programming for me) and wanted to work on an “easier” project.

Too Many Projects:

​ During June or so of this year I worked on and off making an AI cylinder move around some platforms, made an animated character for the player, and messed around with custom physics in Unity of Gravity Zones (zones in which I can lower or raise gravity in a particular zones). It was all fun but I never had any drive to actually do anything with it, so occasionally I add things here and there but I never made a conscious decision to stay and work on it. On top of that I was writing my own music player in Windows for my own music listening pleasure (I love VLC but it doesn’t have the ability to play all music from a specific genre to my knowledge). Then on top of those two projects I was trying to get motion capture from a Kinect a friend gave me to make custom animations for Unity and also face scans of my friend to put into the game. But that effort was quickly shot down after how I couldn’t find a single free solution to get Kinect MoCap data to work in Blender or Unity so I gave up on that, as for the 3D scans I got super hires models but the textures couldn’t be extracted…

To make things even worse, I thought it was a good idea for some reason to try and make an entire Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time remake in unity. Since I thought: “all of the textures, models, sound, and stuff is all existing content so all I need to do it piece it together!” So I worked on the on-screen text stuff, UI, elements, and it was going swimmingly, then you guessed it! Burn Out! That and the thought of all this work for myself wasn’t worth it and a DMCA from Nintendo being already scary. If I can’t share it with everyone online why even do it?

Suffice to say I’ve started too many things and never finished a single one… so this is where my new “plan” comes into fruition. Since I know I go in cycles of inspiration to discouragement I am going to work on a game so simple I couldn’t possibly be hit with massive hurdles like Taurus and my other projects have.

3D Scans of my friend and I in shooter game I was tinkering around in.

Original Taurus:

My idea is making the very original Taurus, not the ones I’ve updated on here the very original one I made a “Development” video of here. The so simple that I didn’t want to originally work on it since it was “too easy” for me back when I had the concept. But little did I know back in 2017 that advancing my ideas for Taurus to make a bigger & better game without actually making a functioning base game is a terrible idea. Since obviously I never have made a finished game before, just working concepts. I explained in an earlier post how my burn out of Taurus was due to my ideas getting bigger and out of my conceptional grasp. This time I’m using my already created ideas back from 2017 in which I have a power-point from when I was in High-School of and making a working simple game. I don’t care when/if i finish it only has a single sale on Steam as long as I have a finished game under my belt that I self-developed and self-published I can sleep soundly at night.

Rapid Fire Boss 3D Design (Unfortunately the model was lost in my HDD failure).

Concluding Thoughts:

My obsession with finishing & publishing a games comes from my personal perception that it’s a really terrible look to want to go into game/software development & not have a single program made. Yes I’ve made simple C++ command prompt games at school & such but those barely count as “completed work” or “work experience”. I want to have a single game that is of my own creation to be finished and published. My perfectionist attitude has been my undoing for years, that and my cycles in inspiration and degradation.

Me messing around with Facebook Messenger filters at work waiting for my bus to arrive.

 I’ll probably disappear for another year or so, but either way I wanted to post here as a therapeutic way to put in “paper” my intentions and get myself into the mood to pull myself out of my development rut. Since this entire summer I haven’t steadily worked on a project unlike 2017 and 2018.

Don’t think that the post is written since I am sad or depressed though, I’m just disappointed in my work ethic towards personal projects. In general life has been really good; so good I’d say it’s been the best 2 years of my life. Having the best job ever as a bus monitor was a welcome surprise, finally being done with math was a weight off my shoulder, and playing/hanging out with friends is always fun. Even now this coming Fall I’m finally learning Japanese academically and not online by myself; which I had a terrible time keeping myself to do consistently.

Either way… bye! Until next time!
