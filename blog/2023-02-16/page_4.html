Being Proactive

Written: 2023-02-16

Fixing My Minecraft Worlds

This one came up relatively recently but was something I thought was impossible for the longest time.

Back in middle school I used to play Minecraft a lot and would always be the one who hosted the worlds for friends to join on. By doing so I had the world files but couldn’t do much since at the time PC and Xbox 360 Minecraft were incompatible with each other. Years went by and I eventually transferred my worlds to my Xbox One edition of the game. Even still I was stuck with them on that version.

Years went by and I always wanted to play them on my PC but knew I couldn’t, since console and PC Minecraft weren’t compatible. However over time Microsoft made updates to the game and made a cross-play version of the game. Minecraft “Bedrock” was a cross-play version of the game that made it so PC and Consoles could play together. I still couldn’t play on my Xbox worlds on PC since on Xbox One there was no way to copy your save files.

Realms

So my worlds were still locked to console no matter what I did, eventually Microsoft introduced a self-hosting service called “Realms” for the game and I was finally able to make that dream become a reality. “Realms” allows players to upload their world file to a Microsoft server so that even if the original owner of the world isn’t online others can play. What this means to me is that I could finally upload my Xbox worlds to the server and download them to PC for the first time ever.

This sounded good an all but all of this stuff was introduced when I didn’t have an Xbox anymore. For the longest time I considered my dream to play my worlds on PC was impossible, and when I stopped using my Xbox I got rid of it. I was distraught but at the beginning of this year I let one of my best friends log into my account on his Xbox and he uploaded the files one-by-one to the server; I downloaded them; and saw old worlds I haven’t played since 2011 and 2012 for the first time in years. It was honestly an emotional moment since some of the worlds were made before I even met some of my best friends I still have today. I was ecstatic and made sure to back them up on my computer.

Normally that’s be the end of it but my plan after I recovered the files was to make a server with a mod that allows multiple worlds to be loaded at once and allows players to teleport to whatever world they want to play on. The problem being is that all my worlds are from the “Bedrock” edition of Minecraft and what I wanted to do was only possible on the “Java” version (these versions are not compatible).

Converting Worlds

What this meant was that I had to find a tool that converted my old worlds to Java ones. Luckily for 80% of them all of them worked without issue, but the last 20% had some issues. In some worlds, creatures like people’s pets were missing, items were missing, and some parts of the worlds loaded incorrectly. So my goal I started this year was to add back in the pets (by comparing the normal “Bedrock” version to the “Java” version), adding back in missing item, and adjusting the conversion tool’s parameters to fixing missing parts.

Currently I’m at a point where 99% of it is done and I have a server my friends can join at any time and go though memory lane to look at the several worlds we played with growing up; all in one central place. The last 1% of what I want to do is add protection to each worlds so people can’t break blocks and/or take items from the worlds (removing the preservation aspect of the world). I doubt my friends would do that since they also want to preserve, but it’s a safety precaution regardless.
