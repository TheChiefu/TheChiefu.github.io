Happy Ball

Happy Ball was an interesting project, not because it was super challenging or too easy but because it deviated from what I generally did in projects. When I first learned how to use Unity when I was around 11 years old I wanted to make something simple to get into learning how to program. So I decided to make a 3D ball rolling game, but limit the axis to the X and Y coordinates so I can make levels easier. But eventually after learning the basics I wanted to make a different game using the new skills and ended up dropping hall ball.

Years later and over the Winter Break of 2020 I wanted to challenge myself to make Happy Ball from scratch after years of learning new programming skills not just in C#, but also C++ and etc. Over the years of learning I found out how to save file data, load from paths, modify game-objects in real time depending on the situation, and etc. So I went full throttle into making Happy Ball. I started from the basics of making a 2D side-scrolling level and made “prefab” objects that I could use in any new level I wanted to create. I setup everything modular so levels could automatically textured based on settings, and give win requirements all from one manager. I didn’t have to hard code anything in and on top of that, the player could customize their look!

Technologies Used: C# & Unity 3D

Console Grid Game

This was a really interesting school project not because it was easy, but because of the limitations and how I had to overcome them. The project specified a 5×5 maze in which the player navigates it to find the treasure hidden within. Hidden within are obstacles in the way to stop the player. The instructor left everything else up to the students. Only to adhere to the main requirements and be creative.

So what I decided to do was challenge myself and create a visual display on the console and draw the grid based on the given Width and Height of it. As well as drawing the player’s position correctly in that grid. The core of the program only took about a week but the last week was just adjusting a 1D string array to draw onto a 2D screen from top left being (0,0) instead of the mathematically traditional (0,0) being center screen.

I wanted to make the program cross-platform with any platform but couldn’t find a suitable solution in time by the deadline to implement screen clearing. I could draw the grid every update, but then it would make the console screen scroll line by line. I wanted to imitate a game instead instead of a console program so I used the Windows screen clearing command. Other than that I was extremely satisfied in getting a drawing solution to exist, and work for any given grid size.

Technologies Used: C++

Taurus – Hobby Project

Taurus is a game I work on in my free-time to really delve deep into creating video games. I started working on this project with one of my school friends during my senior year of high school as part of a game design competition. It started out as a Starfox like game, putting the player on-rails having enemies spawn from the side of the screen. I was tasked with creating 3D models and the playable game while my partner worked on concept art and story ideas.

As time progressed and the competition came, we made a playable tutorial level explaining the details of the game. Once the summer months started in 2017 I worked on the game on/off slowly improving the game until one day my hard-drive failed and had to restart. Currently I work on it when I have the free time, but focus on work over the game. So its a nice side hobby project.

Technologies Used: C# & Unity 3D

Reclamation (Demo)

Reclamation was a video game concept created in my senior year game design class. This project was created within the course of a strict 3 month schedule and our teacher gave us this deadline to give us a realistic perspective of the game development process.

Most of the team was tasked with creating concept art, story creation, and gameplay ideas. I was tasked with taking the concepts and putting the necessary elements into an interactive demo.

As critical as I am with the demo in terms of graphical quality and technical issues, it did help me get more comfortable in the Unity game engine. I was given limited ideas to implement in the time period so I tried to make the best of the situation. It all went well in the end and we presented the concept to our school’s staff and the local news, which wrote an article about our presentation. Unfortunately most of the people who worked on the project after graduation weren’t able to work on the project so it was eventually scrapped.

I did however revisit the demo later on in April of 2022 and optimized it to the best of my ability since it ran so poorly in the past due to inexperience. I called the new version “Reclamation Redux” and wrote about the process in an article on the site!

Technologies Used: C# & Unity 3D
