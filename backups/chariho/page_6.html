Expectation 5: Self-Directed Learner

Experience: Learning the Basics of a new Language [Japanese – 日本語]

While almost all the activities I’ve done over the Summer have been Self-Directed learning, the most interesting and meaningful experience has been the start of a new language learning process. I’ve always wanted to learn Japanese ever since I was a middle school student but, I didn’t have a good way to start learning. I jumped straight into a chart of 46 Japanese Hiragana ⎘ 「ひらがな」and had a hard time learning the basic 5 characters「あ、い、う、え、お」, but was easily discouraged and gave up. But I tried again last summer and made tremendous progress because I found a new site that made me learn it extremely easier. It might have just been because I aged more and found learning to be easier now than before or that before I just wanted to learn it in a day and now I know the process behind it; but either way I still made progress.

Ever since last summer I learn the basic 46 Hiragana 「ひらがな」characters in Japanese. And this summer learned around 30 Japanese words and part of the second character set Katakana 「カタカナ」. The experience has been fun because it is something I’ve wanted to learn for so long. It also makes me want to live abroad more now than ever, now that I know some (I know I can’t live off of what I know now but it make me inspired to learn more). I hope to start the final character set in Japanese, Kanji 「漢字」 a set based off of Chinese characters (being around 2000 characters for literacy). So in addition to the 60+ Japanese characters I know, I hope to learn them as well (maybe through VHS schooling because I want to live Abroad intensely and very much pay for a Japanese learning class).

But the Experience of Learning Japanese Self-Directed has been a blast and I hope to continue learning it during the school year. The helpful website that made me learn Japanese tremendously quick was Memrise ⎘ a free website that lets you choose any user-made course in learning language (such as Spanish and French), learn at your own pace, and reminds you to review words you are doing good or bad on. The words you know better won’t appear until a time period is up known as Spaced Repetition Learning or SRS.

Examples of Sentence I know how to form due to Memrise:

日本語 / Japanese ローマ字 / Romaji 英語 / English
よろしくお願いします Yoroshiku onegai shimasu Nice to Meet You
ありがとうございます Arigato gougozai masu Thank You (Formal)
おはよう Ohayou Good Morning (Informal)
私はオーステンです Watashi wa O-sutenn desu I am Austin

Romaji is a rough sound of how Japanese sounds when read with Roman characters, but is not fully accurate to the Japanese sound system
