Expectation 9: Personal Well-Being

Experience: Exploring New Movies in Japanese Culture

This summer has been an interesting ride, I saw a bunch of movies that I haven’t seen before. All of which were created by the famous animator Hayao Miyazaki.

It all started because I was watching YouTube as I normally do and kept seeing a video appear in the “Recommended for You” section. I originally gave it no thought and made fun of the video because of the funny thumbnail image. However I gave in and finally watched the trailer despite making fun of it.

From there I wanted to know who made it and found out that movies trailers that I’d seen such as “Ponyo” in 2009 were created by the same studio. They were called “Studio Ghibli” and then got interested in their entire Filmography.

Apparently I’d also been a fan of their content without realizing it as well, since I’d been listening to the soundtrack from the movie “Princess Mononoke”, for a while and it was from the same studio. Ever since then I’ve watched 14 movies from their studio and got into all the music.

Movie: The Castle in the Sky 「天空の城ラピュタ」

These movies inspired me to learn Japanese and have made me have the strange effect of bettering my understanding of spoken Japanese since I’d be listening to the language for over an hour (sometimes two). I could pronounce and understand Japanese better, as well as used this time to reinforce the pronunciation and sounding of the language.

The movies also introduced me to a new Musical artist by the name Joe Hisaishi an orchestral conductor and music creator, who creates music for Studio Ghibli. Since I am an avid fan of orchestra I adored all the musical pieces in the movies and listen to his music all the time now.

Experience: Planning for the Future

When the summer rolls around I usually have 2 and half months free to do whatever I can think of. This has it’s downsides due to the fact that most (if not all) of my friends work for most (or entirety) of the summer, so I spend most of my time planning. Odd for a teenager but since I know I only have 2 years of High-school left I better know what I’m doing, to head for the goal I want. When planning I look for what profession I want to enter and at what position. So I generally look at Colleges and places to move to if (or when) I move abroad. My life goal is to move to Japan and become a Game Developer in the position of a Level Designer.

What I’ve learned from Planning is almost exactly what I want to do with my life (of course over time my mind can change), but my set goal is to save money now for college at URI and hopefully take 4 years for my Bachelors degree in Game Design and move to Japan; or even better move abroad to Japan and study there for game design. My dream has always been to live in either Tokyo「東京都」, Osaka 「大阪市」, or even (which would make happier) the rural areas such as the countryside or mountains.
