Chariho Summer

Expectation 7: Global Citizenship

Activity: Help Clean up my Aunt’s Back Yard

It may seem like a small task at first when you think back yards, but since her yard is so big to the point in which it used to have about a 50sq ft. shed with around 2 stories high that was knocked down; it really took a while to clean. The shed had years worth of burning wood and debris that it was a chore to clean up. The back yard is still not fully cleaned due to the fact the shed was so extreme in size that my family and I are still going piece by piece to clean it up. But this summer was by far the most progress because I was outside helping for around a week or two burning day and night giant pieces of wood to clean the area up. Not only was there a tremendous shed in the backyard, but the previous owners of the house used to throw broken glass out, so scattered throughout the yard are tiny and large pieces of glass usually hidden beneath 3 inches of dirt. So while everyone cleans up around the yard like the shed, I usually build camping sites and rake up the leaves in the forest and find the glass. This year I’ve had to find at least 4 full windows total of glass and I helped dispose of it. It was a chore, but since I like cleaning up camp areas it kind of fun because it gives me something to work on while outside and while I’m making a fort.
